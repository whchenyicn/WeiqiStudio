Learning Weiqi is much easier when you can play real games.

You do not need to buy a board immediately. You do not need to find a local club first. You can start online, play short games, review your mistakes, and slowly build confidence.

But there is one problem.

There are many online places to play Weiqi, and they do not all feel the same.

Some are better for beginners.

Some are better for fast games.

Some are better for serious ranked play.

Some have stronger communities, while others feel more competitive or difficult to navigate.

This guide will help you choose the best online place to play Weiqi based on your current level and learning style.

If you are a complete beginner, start here:

| Best For | Recommended Platform |
| --- | --- |
| Complete beginners | OGS |
| English-speaking community | OGS or KGS |
| Fast 9×9 games | GoQuest |

| Learning before playing | Go Magic or beginner lessons first |

For most beginners, the simplest choice is:

**Start with OGS for normal online games, and GoQuest for quick 9×9 games.**

That combination gives you a good balance of learning, practice, and convenience.

## 1. OGS: Best Overall for Beginners

**OGS**, short for Online Go Server, is one of the most beginner-friendly places to play Weiqi online.

It works directly in your browser, so you do not need to install complicated software. You can create an account, find games, play on different board sizes, and review your games afterward.

For beginners, this matters a lot.

When you are still learning liberties, capturing, atari, eyes, and territory, the last thing you want is a confusing interface.

OGS is a good first choice because it feels modern, accessible, and easy to enter.

### Why beginners may like OGS

- Browser-based
- Beginner-friendly interface
- Supports different board sizes
- Good for 9×9, 13×13, and 19×19 games
- Active English-speaking community
- Useful for both live and slower games

### Best use case

Use OGS if you want one main place to play Weiqi online as a beginner.

It is probably the best default recommendation for new English-speaking players.

---

## 2. KGS: Best for Community and Reviews

**KGS** is one of the classic online Go servers.

It has been around for many years and is well known among English-speaking Go players.

KGS is especially valued for its community feeling. Players can watch games, discuss positions, and review games together.

For a beginner, that can be very helpful.

A single review from a stronger player can teach you more than several games played alone.

### Why beginners may like KGS

- Long-standing Go community
- Good for watching and reviewing games
- English-speaking player base
- Useful for teaching games
- Free to use

### Possible downside

KGS may feel less modern than OGS.

If you are used to clean, modern web apps, OGS may feel easier at first.

### Best use case

Use KGS if you want community interaction, reviews, and a more traditional online Go environment.

---

## 3. Pandanet / IGS: Best for Serious Traditional Play

**Pandanet**, also known as IGS, is another long-running online Go server.

It has a more traditional feel and is often associated with serious online play.

Pandanet describes itself as a place where players can play, watch, study, and find opponents of similar skill.

For beginners, Pandanet can be useful, but it may not feel as beginner-friendly as OGS.

The interface and culture may feel more serious.

That is not a bad thing.

It simply means beginners may want to start elsewhere first, then explore Pandanet later.

### Why players may like Pandanet

- Long history
- Serious player base
- Good for ranked games
- International community
- Useful for watching stronger players

### Best use case

Use Pandanet when you are ready for a more serious online playing environment.

It is a good option after you already understand the basics.

---

## 4. GoQuest: Best for Quick 9×9 Games

**GoQuest** is excellent for fast games, especially on a 9×9 board.

This makes it especially useful for beginners.

A full 19×19 game can feel long and overwhelming when you are new. A 9×9 game is shorter, clearer, and gives you more chances to practice the basics.

GoQuest is built around quick online games, so it is convenient when you only have a few minutes.

### Why beginners may like GoQuest

- Great for 9×9 games
- Fast matchmaking
- Mobile-friendly
- Short games
- Good for practicing captures and territory quickly

### Possible downside

Because games are fast, you may play too quickly and miss important lessons.

Use GoQuest for practice, but do not rely on it as your only way to learn.

### Best use case

Use GoQuest when you want quick 9×9 games on mobile.

It is especially helpful for building confidence early.

---

## 5. Fox, Tygem, and WBaduk: Best for Stronger Competition

As you spend more time in the Weiqi world, you may hear about Asian servers such as:

- Fox
- Tygem
- WBaduk

These platforms are popular among many strong players and often have a more competitive atmosphere.

They can be exciting, but they may not be the best first stop for a complete beginner.

The interface may be less beginner-friendly for English speakers, and the playing style can feel intense.

That said, they can be valuable later.

If you want to experience a wider range of opponents and stronger fighting games, these servers are worth exploring once you are more comfortable.

### Best use case

Use these servers later, after you have experience on beginner-friendly platforms.

## 6. Learning Platforms Before Playing

Some beginners should not jump directly into online games.

If you still feel unsure about the rules, it may be better to spend a little time learning first.

Platforms and apps focused on lessons, puzzles, or guided practice can help you understand the basics before you play real opponents.

Look for resources that teach:

- Board and stones
- Liberties
- Capturing
- Atari
- Eyes
- Territory
- Scoring

Once those concepts feel familiar, online games become much less confusing.

At Weiqi Studio, we recommend completing the beginner course before playing too many ranked games.

## What Board Size Should You Play Online?

For beginners, board size matters more than platform.

### Start with 9×9

A 9×9 board is best for your first online games.

Games are short.

Captures happen often.

Mistakes are easier to understand.

You can finish more games and learn faster.

### Move to 13×13

Once 9×9 feels comfortable, try 13×13.

It gives you more strategic space without becoming as overwhelming as 19×19.

### Try 19×19 later

The full board is the standard version of Weiqi.

But you do not need to rush.

If 19×19 feels confusing, return to smaller boards and keep building your fundamentals.

## Should Beginners Play Ranked Games?

You can, but you do not have to.

Ranked games can be useful because they match you with players near your level.

However, they can also create pressure.

If you are brand new, start with casual games first.

Focus on learning:

- How liberties work
- How captures happen
- How to avoid atari
- How to build territory
- When the game is finished

Once you feel more comfortable, ranked games become more useful.

---

## Should You Play Against Bots?

Playing bots can be helpful, especially when you are nervous about playing real people.

Bots are always available.

They do not mind if you make mistakes.

They let you practice at your own pace.

However, bots are not a complete replacement for human opponents.

Humans make beginner-like mistakes.

Humans create emotional pressure.

Humans help you experience the natural rhythm of a real game.

The best approach is to use both.

Play bots for low-pressure practice.

Play humans to learn real game judgment.

## How to Make Online Games More Useful

Playing more games helps, but playing mindlessly is not enough.

To improve faster, use each online game as a learning opportunity.

### After each game, ask three questions

1. Where did I lose stones?
2. Did I miss any atari?
3. Was I building territory or only chasing captures?

These questions are simple, but they will improve your understanding quickly.

### Review one mistake

You do not need to review the entire game.

Choose one mistake and learn from it.

That is enough.

### Play slower than you want to

Fast games are fun, but beginners often improve more from slightly slower games.

Give yourself time to count liberties and look at the whole board.

---

## Common Beginner Mistakes Online

### Playing too fast

Online games can make you rush.

Slow down and think before each move.

### Starting with 19×19 immediately

The full board is exciting, but it can overwhelm beginners.

Start with 9×9.

### Caring too much about rank

Your rank will move up and down.

That is normal.

Focus on learning, not protecting a number.

### Playing without reviewing

If you never review, you may repeat the same mistakes for weeks.

Even short reviews help.

### Switching platforms too often

Choose one main platform at first.

Get comfortable.

Then explore others later.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### What is the best online place to play Weiqi for beginners?

For most English-speaking beginners, OGS is the best starting point because it is browser-based, accessible, and beginner-friendly.

### Is GoQuest good for beginners?

Yes.

GoQuest is especially useful for quick 9×9 games, which are excellent for beginners.

### Should I play on 9×9 or 19×19 online?

Start with 9×9.

Move to 13×13 and 19×19 later when you feel more comfortable.

### Are online ranks accurate?

Online ranks are useful, but they are not perfect.

Different platforms may rank players differently, so do not compare ranks too seriously across servers.

### Can I learn Weiqi only by playing online?

You can learn a lot online, but you will improve faster if you also review games, study basic lessons, and solve beginner problems.
