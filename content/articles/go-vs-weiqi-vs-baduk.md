If you are new to this ancient strategy game, one thing can be confusing right away.

Some people call it Go.

Some call it Weiqi.

Others call it Baduk.

So are these three different games?

No.

Go, Weiqi, and Baduk are three names for the same board game.

The rules, board, stones, and objective are the same. The difference is language, culture, and history.

In this guide, you’ll learn where each name comes from, why English speakers usually say Go, why Weiqi Studio uses the name Weiqi, and which term you should use as a beginner.

## One Game, Three Names

The simplest way to understand it is this:

| Name | Language | Commonly Used In |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Weiqi | Chinese | China and Chinese-speaking communities |
| Go / Igo | Japanese | Japan and much of the English-speaking world |
| Baduk | Korean | Korea and Korean Weiqi communities |

All three names refer to the same two-player strategy board game.

Players take turns placing black and white stones on the intersections of a grid. The goal is to control more territory than your opponent by the end of the game.

So if someone says:

- “I play Go.”
- “I study Weiqi.”
- “I watch Baduk tournaments.”

They are talking about the same game.

## Why Are There Different Names?

The game began in ancient China and later spread to Korea and Japan. As it became part of different cultures, each language developed its own name for the game. The International Go Federation also describes the game as invented in China, where it is known as Wei Qi.

This is not unusual.

Many games and sports have different names in different places.

For example, the same sport may be called football in one country and soccer in another.

The activity is the same.

The name changes depending on language and culture.

Go, Weiqi, and Baduk work the same way.

## What Is Weiqi?

Weiqi is the Chinese name for the game.

In Chinese, it is written as 围棋.

The name is often understood as referring to the idea of surrounding or encircling, which fits the central goal of the game: surrounding territory with your stones.

Weiqi is the name used in China and many Chinese-speaking communities.

At Weiqi Studio, we primarily use this name because it reflects the game’s Chinese origin and also describes the spirit of the game well.

Weiqi is not a different version of Go.

It is the same game.

## What Is Go?

Go is the name most English speakers recognize.

It comes from the Japanese name Igo. In English, the name was commonly shortened to Go.

This is why many English books, websites, clubs, and apps use the word Go instead of Weiqi.

Historically, Japan played a major role in introducing the game to Western countries, so the Japanese-derived name became common in English. Some sources also note that Go reached Japan centuries ago and became deeply developed there through schools, professional systems, and tournament culture.

This creates a practical issue for beginners.

If you search online for “Go,” you may also get results about the English verb “go,” the programming language Go, or unrelated topics.

That is one reason why terms like Weiqi, Baduk, or Go board game can sometimes be easier to search.

## What Is Baduk?

Baduk is the Korean name for the game.

It is written as 바둑 in Korean.

Korea has one of the strongest professional communities in the world, and many top international players have come from Korea.

If you watch Korean tournaments, Korean teaching videos, or Korean professional commentary, you will usually hear the word Baduk.

Again, Baduk is not a separate game.

It is the Korean name for the same game.

## Are the Rules Exactly the Same?

The basic game is the same.

The board, stones, liberties, capturing, eyes, territory, and strategic ideas are shared across Go, Weiqi, and Baduk.

However, there can be small differences in scoring rules depending on the ruleset used.

For example, Chinese rules and Japanese rules count points slightly differently.

For beginners, this does not matter much.

The game you are learning is still the same game.

You do not need to study every scoring variation before you begin.

If you understand territory, capturing, and when the game ends, you already understand the foundation.

## Which Name Should Beginners Use?

Use the name that fits your situation.

### Use “Go” when searching in English

If you are looking for English books, clubs, apps, or online servers, “Go” is still the most common term.

Helpful searches include:

- Go board game
- Learn Go
- Go rules
- Go beginner guide

### Use “Weiqi” when learning from Chinese sources

If your teacher, family, club, or learning materials use Chinese terms, Weiqi will feel more natural.

It also connects directly to the game’s origin.

### Use “Baduk” when following Korean content

If you watch Korean players, Korean tournaments, or Korean teaching channels, Baduk is the term you will see most often.

### Use all three when you want the best search results

For serious learning, it helps to recognize all three names.

A useful beginner search might be:

- Go Weiqi Baduk beginner rules
- Weiqi Go Baduk lessons
- Baduk beginner guide

Knowing all three terms helps you find more resources.

## Why Does Weiqi Studio Use “Weiqi”?

We chose the name Weiqi Studio intentionally.

There are three reasons.

First, Weiqi reflects the game’s Chinese origin.

Second, it is more specific and easier to recognize as a game name than the English word “Go.”

Third, it helps beginners understand that this is not only a game of tactics, but also a game of surrounding, balance, territory, and long-term thinking.

That said, we still mention Go and Baduk throughout the site because many beginners search using those names.

Our goal is simple:

To help complete beginners learn the game clearly, no matter which name they first discover.

## Common Beginner Confusions

### “Is Go different from Weiqi?”

No.

Go and Weiqi are the same game.

Go is the Japanese-derived name commonly used in English.

Weiqi is the Chinese name.

### “Is Baduk harder than Go?”

No.

Baduk is not a harder version of the game.

It is simply the Korean name.

### “Why do apps use different names?”

Different apps are built for different audiences.

An international app may use “Go.”

A Korean app may use “Baduk.”

A Chinese learning platform may use “Weiqi.”

The game itself is the same.

### “Which name is the most correct?”

All three are correct.

The best name depends on context.

Historically, Weiqi reflects the game’s Chinese origin.

Practically, Go is still widely recognized in English.

Culturally, Baduk is the standard Korean name.

## Quick Recap

- Weiqi is the Chinese name.
- Go comes from the Japanese name Igo and is common in English.
- Baduk is the Korean name.
- The basic rules are the same.
- Small scoring differences may exist between rulesets, but beginners do not need to worry about them yet.
- Learning all three names helps you find more books, videos, apps, and communities.
