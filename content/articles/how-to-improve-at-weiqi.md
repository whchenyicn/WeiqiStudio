Every Weiqi player wants to improve.

The good news is that getting better isn't about memorizing thousands of moves or playing like a professional.

Instead, improvement comes from building good habits.

If you're just starting your Weiqi journey, these ten practical tips will help you learn faster, avoid common frustrations, and enjoy the game even more.

## 1. Play Regularly

The simplest way to improve is also the most effective.

Play games consistently.

You don't need to play every day.

Even two or three games each week will help you develop better instincts and become more comfortable with the board.

Consistency matters more than volume.

## 2. Start on a 9×9 Board

Many beginners rush straight to the standard 19×19 board.

While exciting, it can also be overwhelming.

A 9×9 board lets you experience the core ideas of Weiqi—[liberties](/articles/liberties-in-weiqi), [capturing](/articles/how-capturing-works-in-weiqi), [territory](/articles/territory-in-weiqi), and [scoring](/articles/scoring-in-weiqi)—in much shorter games.

You'll learn more from ten completed 9×9 games than from one confusing 19×19 game.

## 3. Count Liberties Constantly

Strong players don't guess.

They count.

Before every move, quickly count:

- Your liberties.
- Your opponent's liberties.

This habit helps you avoid unnecessary losses and spot tactical opportunities.

## 4. Review Every Game

Playing teaches you experience.

Reviewing teaches you understanding.

After each game, ask yourself:

- Where did I lose stones?
- Did I miss an atari?
- Was there a better direction to play?

Even a five-minute review can teach valuable lessons.

## 5. Don't Fear Losing

Every beginner loses.

In fact, losing is one of the fastest ways to improve.

Instead of asking:

"Did I win?"

Ask:

"What did I learn?"

That mindset will keep you improving long after others become discouraged.

## 6. Look at the Whole Board

One of the best habits you can develop is pausing before every move.

Instead of immediately responding to the nearest fight, scan the entire board.

Ask yourself:

- Where is the biggest move?
- Which group needs attention?
- Is there a better opportunity elsewhere?

This habit separates beginners from stronger players.

## 7. Learn Principles Before Memorization

Many new players search for opening patterns or professional games.

There's nothing wrong with that—but only after you've mastered the fundamentals.

Understanding why a move works is far more valuable than memorizing where someone else played.

Strong fundamentals will help you in every position.

## 8. Solve Simple Problems

Life-and-death puzzles are one of the best ways to improve your reading ability.

Start with beginner-level problems.

Focus on accuracy rather than speed.

Even solving a few puzzles each week can significantly sharpen your tactical skills.

## 9. Watch Stronger Players

Watching experienced players can expose you to new ideas.

Don't worry if you don't understand every move.

Instead, pay attention to the overall flow of the game.

Notice:

- How they build territory.
- When they attack.
- When they defend.
- When they choose not to fight.

Over time, these patterns become familiar.

## 10. Enjoy the Journey

Perhaps the most important advice is this:

Don't rush.

Weiqi is a lifelong game.

Even professionals continue learning throughout their careers.

Celebrate small improvements.

Every game, every review, and every mistake brings you one step closer to becoming a stronger player.

## Improvement Takes Time

Many beginners ask:

"How long will it take before I become good?"

The honest answer is that improvement never truly ends.

Fortunately, that's one of the reasons people continue enjoying Weiqi for decades.

There is always another idea to discover.

Another mistake to learn from.

Another game to enjoy.

## Quick Recap

To improve faster:

- Play consistently.
- Start small.
- Count liberties.
- Review your games.
- Learn from losses.
- Look at the whole board.
- Understand principles.
- Solve simple puzzles.
- Watch stronger players.
- Enjoy the journey.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### How often should beginners play?

Two or three games each week is enough to make steady progress.

Consistency is more important than playing many games in a short period.

### Is playing stronger opponents helpful?

Yes.

You'll probably lose more often, but you'll also learn much faster.

### Should I study professional games?

Eventually, yes.

However, beginners usually benefit more from mastering the basic principles first.

### What's the fastest way to improve?

Play games, review them, and keep learning from your mistakes.

There is no shortcut that replaces experience.
