Every strong player started exactly where you are today.

## Step 1: Start Small

Although professional games are played on a 19×19 board, we recommend starting on a 9×9 board.

A smaller board helps you:

- Finish games more quickly.
- Practice capturing more often.
- Understand territory more easily.
- Learn from your mistakes without feeling overwhelmed.

There's no rush to move to the full-sized board.

Building confidence on a 9×9 board is the fastest way to improve.

## Step 2: Focus on the Fundamentals

During your first few games, forget about advanced strategy.

Instead, ask yourself a few simple questions before every move:

- Does this stone have enough liberties?
- Can I connect to my other stones?
- Is my opponent in atari?
- Am I protecting my own groups?
- Am I building territory?

If you can answer these questions consistently, you're already playing real Weiqi.

## Step 3: Don't Chase Every Capture

Capturing stones feels exciting.

Many beginners spend the entire game trying to surround every enemy stone they see.

Try not to fall into that habit.

Remember:

The goal is to control territory—not collect captures.

Sometimes the strongest move is simply expanding into open space or strengthening one of your own groups.

## Step 4: Accept Mistakes

You will lose stones.

You will miss atari.

You will accidentally help your opponent.

That is completely normal.

Every mistake teaches you something.

In fact, reviewing your mistakes after each game is one of the fastest ways to improve.

Don't judge your progress by whether you win.

Judge it by whether you understand the game a little better than yesterday.

## Step 5: Look at the Whole Board

One piece of advice experienced players often give beginners is surprisingly simple:

Before every move, look at the whole board.

It's easy to become absorbed in one local fight.

However, Weiqi is a game of balance.

Sometimes the biggest opportunity isn't where you're currently looking.

Developing the habit of stepping back and seeing the entire board will improve both your strategy and your decision-making.

## Step 6: Enjoy the Process

That means you never need to feel "behind."

The joy of Weiqi comes from the journey, not just the result.

## Common Beginner Mistakes

### Expecting to win immediately

Improvement in Weiqi happens gradually.

Focus on learning, not your win rate.

### Playing too quickly

Take a moment before every move.

Count liberties.

Look at the whole board.

Think about your plan.

### Memorizing instead of understanding

Learning principles is far more valuable than memorizing sequences of moves.

Strong understanding will serve you in every game.

### Being afraid to lose

Every loss is a lesson.

Many experienced players improve more from the games they lose than the games they win.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### Am I ready to play online?

Yes.

A beginner-friendly 9×9 game is the perfect next step.

Don't worry about your rating or your results.

Focus on learning.

### How often should I play?

Consistency is more important than quantity.

Even a few games each week will help you improve steadily.

### Should I review my games?

Absolutely.

Reviewing your own games is one of the fastest ways to identify mistakes and discover better ideas.

### When should I move to a 19×19 board?

Once you're comfortable with the basic rules and can consistently finish 9×9 games, you'll be ready to explore larger boards.

There's no fixed timeline.

Everyone learns at their own pace.
