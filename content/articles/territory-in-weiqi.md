By now, you've learned how stones survive, how groups are captured, and why eyes are important.

But one question remains:

How do you actually win a game of Weiqi?

The answer is simple.

You win by controlling more territory than your opponent.

Many beginners believe the player who captures the most stones wins.

That isn't true.

Capturing stones can help you gain territory, but territory—not captures—is what decides the winner.

## What Is Territory?

Territory is the empty space that only one player can safely control.

Imagine building a fence around a piece of land.

The land inside the fence belongs to you because your opponent cannot enter without being captured.

In Weiqi, your stones act like that fence.

As they surround empty intersections, they create territory.

At the end of the game, these controlled intersections become part of your final score.

## Territory Is Empty Space

This surprises many beginners.

Your stones themselves are not territory.

The empty intersections they surround are.

For example, if your stones completely surround a small area containing four empty intersections, those four points become your territory.

Thinking this way helps explain why players spread their stones across the board instead of filling every space.

## Bigger Isn't Always Better

It may seem tempting to surround a huge area as quickly as possible.

However, large territory is only valuable if it can be defended.

If your walls are weak or incomplete, your opponent may invade and reduce your territory.

Strong players look for balance.

They try to build areas that are both large and secure.

Sometimes a smaller, guaranteed territory is worth more than a larger area that cannot be defended.

## Corners Are Easier to Control

Earlier, you learned that games often begin near the corners.

Now you can see why.

The edge of the board acts like a natural wall.

In a corner, two sides are already protected by the edge of the board.

This means you need fewer stones to surround territory.

Along the side, you need more stones.

In the center, you need even more.

This simple idea explains many opening moves in Weiqi.

## Territory Changes Throughout the Game

One important lesson for beginners is that territory is not permanent until the game is over.

An area that looks safe early in the game may later be invaded.

Likewise, an area that seems small can grow into a large territory through good play.

Don't worry if the board looks messy during the middle game.

Territory becomes clearer as the game develops.

## Territory vs Influence

As you improve, you'll hear another important word:

Influence.

Territory is land you already control.

Influence is your potential to control areas in the future.

Strong players balance both.

Sometimes they choose secure territory.

Other times they sacrifice immediate points to build influence across a larger area of the board.

For now, simply remember:

- Territory scores points today.
- Influence creates opportunities for tomorrow.

## Why Capturing Isn't Everything

Capturing stones feels satisfying.

It removes your opponent's stones from the board and often creates new opportunities.

However, chasing every possible capture can actually hurt your position.

Imagine capturing three stones while your opponent quietly surrounds twenty empty intersections elsewhere.

Even though you made the exciting move, your opponent may still finish with a higher score.

Always ask yourself:

"Does this move help me control more territory?"

That question will lead to much stronger decisions.

## Common Beginner Mistakes

### Counting stones instead of territory

The winner is determined by territory, not by who has more stones on the board.

### Building territory too early

Large areas without strong boundaries can often be invaded later.

Build steadily rather than trying to claim the whole board at once.

### Ignoring the corners

Corners are usually the easiest places to create secure territory.

This is why most games begin there.

### Chasing captures

Capturing is only valuable if it improves your overall position.

Sometimes expanding your territory is the stronger move.

## Quick Recap

Before moving on, remember these key ideas:

- Territory is empty space surrounded by your stones.
- The goal of Weiqi is to control more territory than your opponent.
- Capturing helps, but territory wins games.
- Corners are the easiest places to build territory.
- Territory can change throughout the game.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### Do my stones count as territory?

No.

The stones help create territory, but the empty intersections they surround are what count.

### Can territory be invaded?

Yes.

Until the game is finished, territory can often be reduced or invaded if it is not well defended.

### Why do professional games start in the corners?

Because corners require fewer stones to surround territory, making them the most efficient places to begin.

### Should I always build territory instead of attacking?

Not always.

Good players balance attack, defense, territory, and influence depending on the position.
